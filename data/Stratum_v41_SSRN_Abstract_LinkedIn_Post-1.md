# Stratum v41 - SSRN Abstract and LinkedIn 3-1-1 Post

## SSRN Abstract

This paper develops Stratum v41, a state-based framework for measuring systemic instability as a synchronization failure under constraint rather than as a linear response to exogenous shocks. The central state variable is the Synchronization Gap (SG), which captures the distance between pressure at systemically relevant chokepoints and the system's effective coordination capacity. We combine this construct with the Information Saturation Index (ISI), estimated from synchronization gaps, friction accumulation, and systemic synchronization. Empirical evidence from the Stratum Engine identifies a structural threshold, tau = 0.3492, beyond which heterogeneous trajectories cease to remain bounded and execution outcomes become state-dependent.

We formalize this result as execution non-invariance: in high-ISI regimes, identical nominal conditions do not guarantee identical outcomes. The framework is validated across energy chokepoints, sovereign stress transmission, rare earth element supply chains, and dynamic portfolio allocation. We show that systemic instability is node-dependent, not shock-dependent: only disruptions affecting high-systemic-relevance nodes activate transmission when absorption capacity is insufficient. Policy implications follow directly: financial stability should be managed as a coordination-capacity problem, not merely as a volatility or liquidity problem. Capital allocation should therefore shift from static diversification to state-contingent convex defense.

**Keywords:** systemic risk, synchronization gap, information saturation, chokepoints, local projections, convex defense, geopolitical risk, rare earth elements, sovereign stress.

**JEL:** E44, F51, G01, G11, G15, H63, Q43.

## LinkedIn 3-1-1 Post

### 3 observations

1. Modern crises are not simply shock events. They are synchronization failures under constraint.

2. The critical variable is not volatility. It is whether the system still has enough coordination capacity to absorb pressure at key nodes.

3. In high information-saturation regimes, identical nominal conditions no longer produce identical outcomes. Execution becomes state-dependent.

### 1 implication

Risk management must move from static diversification to state-contingent exposure. The relevant question is not "what is the expected return?" but "what state is the system in?"

### 1 closing thesis

Systemic risk does not diversify away. It must be detected before the absorption channel saturates.
